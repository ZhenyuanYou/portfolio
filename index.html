Smart Travel Planner — AI Lab
AI Lab Activity  ·  2025  ·  ChatGPT GPT Builder
✓ Completed

In this AI Lab, I explored generative AI tools and applied design thinking to build a practical assistant. I created the Smart Travel Planner — a chatbot that helps university students with limited budgets plan trips efficiently, generating structured itineraries, destination recommendations, and budget-friendly travel advice.

Objective
Design a user-centered AI assistant for budget-conscious university students planning affordable trips.
Tools Used
ChatGPT · GPT Builder · Prompt Engineering
Target Audience
University students with limited travel budgets.
Live Assistant
→ Try Smart Travel Planner
Value Proposition

This project demonstrates my ability to apply AI tools creatively to real-world problems. It showcases user-centered design thinking, prompt engineering skills, and the capacity to iterate and improve an AI system through structured testing — competencies critical in the AI/ML field.

Design Thinking Process
1
Empathy — Identified university students with limited budgets as the primary user group. Travel planning is time-consuming and overwhelming for students who lack experience and financial resources.
2
Define — Problem statement: "University students often want to travel but struggle to plan affordable trips because of limited budgets and the time required to research travel options."
3
Ideate — Designed the Smart Travel Planner to recommend destinations, generate itineraries, suggest budget-friendly attractions and restaurants, and provide money-saving travel tips.
4
Prototype — Built using GPT Builder. Configured to prioritize budget strategies — hostels, street food, public transportation — and added daily cost estimates for better budget management.
5
Test — Validated with prompts such as "Plan a cheap 7-day trip to Japan" and "Best cheap countries for students to travel." The assistant produced structured itineraries with activities, food, transport, and daily cost estimates.
Key Insight: Prompt Engineering

During experimentation, I observed that generative AI produces dynamic responses rather than fixed answers — the same prompt yields different outputs each time. More critically, providing clearer context and constraints dramatically improved response quality, reinforcing prompt engineering as a core professional skill.

Dynamic Outputs Context Drives Quality Iterative Refinement User-Centered Design
Future Improvements

Future versions could integrate real-time flight prices, hotel availability, and transportation schedules. Additional features — packing lists, travel safety advice, and personalized recommendations based on user preferences — would further enhance the assistant's utility.
